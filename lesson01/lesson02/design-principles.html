Proximity

Bloomberg Asia

www.bloomberg.com

I believe the designer of this page grouped the page into three parts; the top, the middle, and the bottom. The brand name, the settings icon, and the "subscribe" text were placed on top. The top part of the page is the common place to put the brand name and the settings icon so it also involves consistency; they just added the subscribe text to fill the white space there. These three elements were grouped at the top but are separated which tells us that they are not really connected to each other and have different uses on the page.

In the middle part, we can see that the image in the center, the word "Market", and the headline were grouped together which tells us that these are related elements. Looking closely, we can see that this group is the featured article on the page and that it is related to the word "Market" (which surely means "stock market" because Bloomberg is a business-related company).

Lastly, at the bottom, we see three lines of texts which the designer did a good job of putting dotted lines to separate them from the headline text above it.

Overall, the designer was able to apply proximity on the page creating a nice, readable page.

White Space

Skype

www.skype.com

This webpage from Skype is one good example of managing white spaces and creating a clean design out of it. As we look at the picture, there's really nothing much here. The owner of this website just wants us to click the "Create a free meeting" button in the middle (Nowadays, remote working is really getting popular because of the COVID19 situation around the world), the "Host A Meeting" linked text which is somewhat similar, and the "Download Skype" linked text.

You can only do 3 things on this webpage mainly and that is too much whitespace. In order to solve this, the designer added some texts and an image as an inviting factor. The designer added some spaces between each elements and the font sizes were bigger so the page were completely covered in the end. Another thing that I like in this design is that the elements were in the center of the page so some of the white spaces were pushed to the edges thus created a nice clean look.

In conclusion, I learned a very useful technique on this page on how to manage white spaces when I have very few elements to work on.

Contrast

Ferrari

www.ferrari.com

I was trying to look for a webpage that sells high-end products to find nice pictures and came across Ferrari. As soon as I saw this webpage, I knew that this is a good design.

Regarding contrast, the image had a darker shadow design on the top and the bottom which made the texts standout. The texts where white therefore stood out on the dark image. Lastly, the car in the middle had a lighter color. This added a visual effect of making our eyes focus on the center of the image which was surely the goal of the designer.

Overall, this is a good example of contrast when designing a webpage and we all should learn from it.
